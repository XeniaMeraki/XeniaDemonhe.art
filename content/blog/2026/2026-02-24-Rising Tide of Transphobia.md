{{ indent (n=2) }}There has always been transphobia around me ever since I was an adult capable of understanding people and politeness and social justice. It was on forums, comment sections, it was in movies and TV shows. Though I would not understand the media aspect of transphobia until I actually transitioned. And then rewatched certain clips in a new light, understood just what the joke of laughing at men in skirts would accomplish. 

[![Alt text](https://files.catbox.moe/c4qhcq.png)](https://files.catbox.moe/c4qhcq.png "A frame of animation from City: The Animation episode 1, of 3 characters laughing at a man in a skirt.")

{{ indent (n=2) }}I was against bigotry early on, and seeked to understand how to be polite and kind to gay and trans people alike. I think I knew early on that trans people faced a lot of prejudice, it was much the same of gay and bi people hatred to high school me. I knew early on it was mean and cruel to be mean to a trans person in regards to their transness. Treat people how you wish to be treated was taught to me and imprinted on me at a very young age. The Golden Rule. I have wondered sometimes why that was so unique to my schooling. I knew vaguely of a trans man that had newly transitioned in my highschool, but never spoke to him directly or was friends with him before or after. I found out I was bi in highschool, I was always rather progressive, most likely said some stupid and wrong things as a teenager still though. I found out I was trans around 29 years old, 2 years of HRT now and soon to be 33.

{{ indent (n=2) }}My lived experience of growing to know and accept trans people, and becoming trans myself, finds cis people wanting whenever they act like they are too young or too old or in too different of a culture to possibly understand trans people or how to be kind to trans people. *I understood how to be kind to trans people at the ripe age of 16 you fucking assholes. With my parents and my school teachers having taught me nothing.*

{{ indent (n=2) }}On a forum I frequented there was an incident where during the making of a visual novel dating sim, featuring the characters of the forum goers themselves, one high profile member had argued that a trans member's gender should be the gender they were before for the game, not the one they are now. Or some such bigoted thing, I do not remember the details mostly. Despite thousands of posts submitted to the forum, that member was banned and would be infamous for being a transphobe. Forever after.

{{ indent (n=2) }}Transphobia has ramped up in previous years it feels like and it turns out. There was always transphobia, no doubt. The nazis burning of The Institute of Sexology in 1933. But the news cycle, living in the USA in 2026, the republicans in general being in power, both in administration and in a great many states, means they can push whatever they so desire. And they desire to push hatred. White hot, scarring, mind destroying, hatred. I know that at least in politics and in terms of law and in terms of Press, transphobia has ramped up to the maximum. It is cloyying. It is ever present. It is suffocating. It is endless. And it is so fucking annoying.

{{ indent (n=2) }}In terms of online culture, the everyday person, transphobia may be getting slightly worse. But in my online circles at least we are always cushioned against experiencing transphobia. Discords and message boards and SOME subreddits are a much needed escape from transphobia. Even the lightest liberal tends to feel that transphobia has no place in polite conversation. A general ruleset banning bigotry of any kind; racism, sexism, transphobia, tends to make most online spaces halfway pleasant if not more. To be honest such things are the only thing that keeps trans people sane I feel. To be surrounded by transphobia in media, press, by family, by folks walking by on the street, but to receive a reprieve from the hate when going into nerdy and lefty spots online. I am not myself in my waking physical reality, but I am surrounded by love and care and well-wishers online, who see me for the real me.
